Strategy Scroll

The 5-Minute Rule

If you're avoiding a task, commit to doing it for just 5 minutes. Often, starting is the hardest part!

Body Doubling

Work alongside someone else, even if you're not doing the same task. Their presence can help maintain focus.

External Reminders

Use alarms, sticky notes, or calendar alerts for everything. Don't rely solely on your memory.

Task Batching

Group similar small tasks together (e.g., answer all emails at once) to reduce context switching.

Brain Dump

Got a distracting thought? Write it down immediately on a dedicated list to address later. (See Brain Dump panel!)

Time Blocking

Schedule specific blocks of time for important tasks in your calendar, treating them like appointments.

Pomodoro Technique

Work in focused 25-minute intervals, followed by a 5-minute break. Longer break after 4 Pomodoros.

Clear Workspace

A tidy desk reduces visual distractions. Keep only what you need for the current task in front of you.

Noise Management

Use noise-cancelling headphones or white noise to minimize auditory distractions and improve concentration.
